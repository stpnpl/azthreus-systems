Interstellar Software Architecture Consulting

Designing software for deep space is categorically different from terrestrial engineering. Round-trip communication delays measured in minutes or hours, cosmic radiation that corrupts memory at random, century-scale operational lifetimes, and a near-total inability to patch deployed systems demand architectural choices that cannot be made intuitively.

Azthreus Systems brings deep expertise in hybrid quantum-classical architecture, delay-tolerant network design, and radiation-hardened systems engineering to organizations navigating these constraints. Whether you are designing a new spacecraft flight stack, building ground control infrastructure, or planning a multi-planetary communications relay — we help you make the right architectural decisions before the first line of code is written.

Our consulting engagements follow the Azthreus hybrid framework: agile iterative development combined with formal aerospace gate reviews (PDR, CDR, TRR, FRR), Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) for every major technical choice, and rigorous requirements traceability from system-level objectives to individual modules.

Service 02

Mission-Critical Verification & Validation

For safety-critical space software, testing cannot be an afterthought — it must be designed into the development lifecycle from the earliest phases. Independent Verification and Validation (IV&V) per NASA NPR 7150.2D is the gold standard for ensuring that flight software does what it is specified to do, and nothing more.

Azthreus Systems provides fully independent V&V teams — engineers who had no role in the development of the software under review. This independence is mandatory for Class A (Mission-Critical) software and ensures that the validation is not contaminated by the assumptions and blind spots of the development team.

Our V&V process includes formal gate reviews (PDR, CDR, TRR, FRR) with mandatory exit criteria, hardware-in-the-loop testing where applicable, adversarial and out-of-distribution test case design, FDIR validation across all documented failure modes, and static analysis using MISRA C:2012, Clippy, SonarQube, and Coverity with zero tolerance for unresolved High or Critical findings at TRR.

"A model or system that produces wrong outputs 5% of the time is not acceptable when those outputs govern docking maneuvers, life-support adjustment, or emergency habitat pressure."

V&V Coverage

Unit Testing Integration Testing System Testing Hardware-in-the-Loop Fault Injection FDIR Validation Adversarial Testing Static Analysis CVE / SBOM Scanning

Gate Reviews

  • PDR — Architecture & requirements baseline
  • CDR — Detailed design with interface sign-off
  • TRR — Code complete, zero critical static analysis findings
  • FRR — Full test suite pass, mission simulation, kill-switch verified

Standards

  • NASA NPR 7150.2D
  • MISRA C:2012 mandatory ruleset
  • OWASP Top 10 / CWE Top 25
  • NIST SP 800-53

Service 03

Deep Space AI & Automated Governance

Autonomous AI systems in space are not optional — communication delays make human-in-the-loop decision-making impossible for time-critical events. Autonomous docking, life-support management, habitat pressure control, and emergency response must all be handled by onboard intelligence that performs correctly every time.

Azthreus Systems designs, validates, and governs AI systems across four Risk Tiers: T1 (Autonomous Mission-Critical, no human approval required), T2 (Human-Supervised Mission-Relevant), T3 (Operational Support), and T4 (Research/Experimental). Each tier carries corresponding requirements for independent IV&V, explainability, bias auditing, and the operational kill-switch that is mandatory for all T1 systems.

Our Ethical AI Review Board (EARB) — composed of technical AI experts, ethicists, legal representatives, and mission operations personnel — reviews every T1 and T2 system before deployment. This is not a checkbox. The EARB has authority to block deployment, require additional testing, and escalate unresolved ethical concerns to the Executive Board.

We also design the governance structures your organization needs: algorithmic transparency reports, bias audit programs, human override mechanisms that cannot be circumvented, and monitoring dashboards that detect performance drift before it becomes a mission risk.

AI Risk Tiers

  • T1 — Autonomous Mission-Critical: Full IV&V, FRR gate, kill-switch mandatory
  • T2 — Human-Supervised: CDR + TRR, explainability required, human override
  • T3 — Operational Support: Standard quality + bias audit pre-deployment
  • T4 — Research: Documentation only; reclassify before deployment
